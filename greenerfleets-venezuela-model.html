Parts pre-position ($18.5M) is funded by Fiducia and managed through Dieselval — a relationship asset for GreenerFleets, not a GF capital outlay. The remaining ~5% of $250M provides buffer for cost overruns, loss provisions, and opportunistic fills.
Who pays GF — and for what
Fiducia Capital pays GF the all-in port-lot price per unit — acquisition + all prep costs + GF margin. Fiducia is the single paying counterparty on asset transactions. The end operator in Venezuela never sees GF's margin.
Program funder (DFC, Ex-Im, state grants) pays GF the program administration fee — 8–12% of capital administered. Entirely separate from the truck price; does not inflate asset cost to Fiducia or the end operator.
Dieselval Valencia pays GF a referral/distribution commission on parts flowing to the Cummins fleet. End operator pays Dieselval's normal price — GF earns from Dieselval's margin. This stream is earned by designing the fleet correctly upfront.
The competitive price test: Chinese new trucks land in Venezuela at $79K–$105K. GF must deliver Fiducia a Cummins-powered US used truck at a port-lot price that — after Fiducia adds shipping and in-country costs — remains competitive or is clearly justified on total cost of ownership. The Cost Stack tab shows exactly where this holds.
Scope boundary — stops at the port lot: GF delivers a staged, inspected, upfitted, GL-52-documented asset at AIT Mobile or equivalent Gulf RoRo staging lot. Ocean carrier, shipping insurance, and in-country logistics are outside GF's scope. The shipping lane needs an owner in the project group — Knut is the natural candidate.

Capital structure options — ranked by preference
Option A · BestFiducia pre-funding escrow per confirmed tranche
Fiducia confirms a tranche (e.g. 50 units by spec), puts funds in escrow, GF draws per unit as it stages at AIT. Float exposure drops to near zero — GF is buying on Fiducia's committed capital.
Pilot tranche (10–20 units) establishes the workflow template. Once proven, per-tranche escrow becomes the permanent standard structure.
Counter to Fiducia hesitation: FOB handoff means they own the asset the moment it stages at AIT — the escrow is timing, not incremental exposure.
Option B · Preferred fallbackEquipment finance line — purchase-for-export basis
Matt Meehan structures a revolving line with a specialty lender — purchase-for-export, no title/registration required. Lender advances against confirmed Fiducia purchase orders or letters of credit.
Start at $1.5M for pilot, negotiate increases at each ramp stage tied to Fiducia's confirmed demand. Finance cost ($360–$810/unit) is built into the per-unit cost stack and recovered in GF's price to Fiducia.
Option C · Future stateDFC or Ex-Im Bank credit facility
Once Year 1 transactions are documented, GF applies for a DFC or Ex-Im facility. Government-backed below-prime lending reduces finance cost per unit by 2–3 percentage points — material at $10M+ revolving.
Mike's government network and Sanford's OFAC compliance documentation are the inputs to this application. 12–18 months to establish. Phase 2–3 capital, not Phase 1.

GF stops at the port lot. These are the relationships that make that stopping point work — and where responsibility passes to others in the project.

Primary — AutoMobile International Terminal · Port of Mobile, Alabama
Why AIT Mobile is the right primary relationship
7,000 vehicle on-site storage capacity — GF's staging lot is already built. No separate yard lease needed once the relationship is established.
VPC with 25 workstations and PDI inspection tunnels — GF's final inspection and upfit work happens here. Potentially eliminates a separate upfit yard entirely.
8,000 sq.m of concrete for high and heavy operations — Class 7 and Class 8 trucks specifically accommodated. AIT has already discussed this capacity with commercial equipment manufacturers.
VPC can convert to Equipment Processing Centre (EPC) — AIT understands commercial truck processing. GF is not an unusual customer for this facility.
Connected to five Class 1 rail lines — trucks can be railed into AIT from anywhere in the Southeast as volume scales past 50 units/month.
Dedicated RoRo berths, 457m length, accommodates any PCTC vessel. $60M joint venture between Alabama State Port Authority and AIT — a serious, permanent facility.
First call: AIT operations team — Leonardo Montenegro, COO. Commercial account for used commercial truck staging, VPC access, high/heavy processing. Ask for per-unit rate for staging + VPC processing and minimum volume for preferred pricing. The Venezuela fleet program makes this a growing, sticky account.

RoRo terminal operated by Ports America. Close to Houston truck supply base — units sourced from Texas dealers and auctions can be driven or hauled directly. Ports America handles new and used vehicles, construction and agricultural machinery, and heavy equipment.

Both are Ports America RoRo operations on the Texas Gulf coast with direct Gulf access and heavy cargo capability. Useful as overflow options when AIT has scheduling constraints, or when Texas-heavy sourcing months make Gulf routing more efficient.

Key headwinds — severity rated, hedges specified. All risks identified through development of this model.

HIGH
Bridge capital gap — 45-day float at scale
At 65 units/month average, GF has $7–9M continuously deployed paying sellers before Fiducia pays at the port lot. Running out of line stalls the pipeline and damages the Fiducia relationship. This risk materializes at Month 7 if the line is not in place beforehand.
Hedge: Matt Meehan establishes the $1.5M acquisition line before the first unit is purchased. Pilot transaction documents the template lenders need. Fiducia per-tranche escrow is negotiated as the permanent solution — it eliminates float risk entirely. Line is sized ahead of each ramp increase, not in response to it.
HIGH
OFAC / GL-52 compliance failure
Venezuela remains under US sanctions. One non-compliant transaction — wrong counterparty, missing documentation, wrong end-use certification — stops the entire operation and creates personal founder exposure. Enforcement is active.
Hedge: Export legal counsel on every unit in Phase 1 ($300/unit, built into cost stack). Align GF transaction documentation format with Sanford's OFAC platform as it matures — his foreign affairs database is the compliance infrastructure GF benefits from without building. Never skip legal review to save per-unit cost.
HIGH
Single-buyer concentration — Fiducia Capital
If Diego Terrero and Roberto's capital availability stalls, a key end-customer fails, or the relationship deteriorates, GF has no buyer for its pipeline. At $237M over 48 months, Fiducia failure is existential.
Hedge: Knut builds a direct Shell Venezuela relationship — a buyer path that does not route through Fiducia. Per-tranche escrow limits exposure to one batch at a time. Identify a secondary Venezuelan capital firm as backup by Month 18, initially at a 90/10 split.
MED
Cat 1 light duty margin compression
At $2,500 flat fee per unit, Cat 1 is the lowest-margin category and most volume-dependent. Chinese alternatives ($25K–$41K new) undercut GF's Fiducia price significantly on sticker. Fiducia may push back or divert Cat 1 demand to Chinese suppliers.
Hedge: Lead every Cat 1 conversation with the Cummins 6.7L Dieselval parts story, 8–12 year Venezuela-adjusted service life, and manual transmission availability. Build a one-page TCO model per unit type. The Chinese sticker advantage disappears at Year 2 when parts cost is factored in.
MED
Chinese competition across all categories
Sinotruk, Foton, JAC, and Dongfeng are actively selling new trucks in Venezuela. Without government shipping support, Chinese trucks can appear more affordable on first cost. The Chinese presence is growing — the window to establish US-supply loyalty is not unlimited.
Hedge: Push DFC/Ex-Im pricing support through Mike's government network. In the near term, the Cummins platform story (Dieselval, 55–65% SKU reduction, single fluid spec) is GF's competitive differentiation. Focus Fiducia's early demand on Cat 2 and Cat 5 where GF has no credible Chinese competitor, to establish the US-supply relationship before competing on tighter categories.
MED
Political & policy risk — US and Venezuela
A US administration change, tightening of Venezuela sanctions, or Maduro policy shift could suspend or complicate the program. Trucks for Trade Act has no congressional champion yet. GL-52 is granted, not permanent — the license can be modified or revoked.
Hedge: Build the business to work without government shipping support and without Trucks for Trade. Government support is modeled as upside only. Use DFC + Ex-Im as the near-term government pathway (no legislation required). Monitor GL-52 status continuously — legal counsel flags any OFAC policy changes as they occur.
MED
AIT staging cost uncertainty and scheduling risk
GF's port-lot model depends on AIT's cooperation on rate, scheduling, and VPC access. If AIT prioritizes OEM volume over GF's smaller used commercial batches, staging delays extend the float window and add holding cost.
Hedge: Establish the AIT relationship in Phase 1 before it's needed at scale. Negotiate a written staging agreement with per-unit rates and scheduling commitments. Identify secondary Texas terminals so AIT knows GF has alternatives — negotiating leverage even as a smaller account.
MED
Ramp pace slower than modeled
If Fiducia's loan book builds more slowly than expected, monthly volume could stay at 20–30 units longer — reducing revenue and making the capital line expensive relative to utilization.
Hedge: Phase 1 is intentionally light on fixed cost. Fixed headcount only scales when Fiducia's confirmed demand justifies it. The capital line should be a revolving facility, not a term loan — underutilization does not create a fixed payment problem.
LOW
Non-Cummins units entering the procurement pipeline
Price or availability pressure could push a sourcing coordinator toward a non-Cummins unit. Every exception erodes parts standardization and the Dieselval relationship that the parts tail revenue depends on.
Hedge: Cummins-only rule is written into supplier agreements and Fiducia demand specifications as an eligibility requirement, not a preference. Any exception requires founder approval and Dieselval parts confirmation for that specific engine. No exceptions in Phase 1.
